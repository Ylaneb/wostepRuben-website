Balance Hairspring Manufacturing

Theory and practice of assembling watch regulating organs

Mastering the design and shaping the geometry of a metal alloy hairspring

Training objectives

  • Balance Hairspring Manufacturing course allows participants to develop practice and knowledge in precision timing and its intricacies.

Prerequisites

The Balance Hairspring Manufacturing course is open to all participants who have attended a watchmaking school and have a complementary experience in watch repairs. 

Watchmakers with no equivalent educational background or training, must have a minimum of 3 years full-time experience in watchmaking.

The participants need to possess the fundamentals skills and experience as performed in the Chronometry AdjustmentsWOSTEP course.

We strongly stress the fact that these requirements are necessary in order to fully benefit from the Balance Hairspring Manufacturing WOSTEP course. 

Program

Main topics

Assembly

  • Regulating organs assembly
  • Fitting a new balance staff

Adjustments

  • Performing the truing and poising of balance wheels
  • Colleting, counting and shaping a metal alloy hairspring
  • Using Leroy’s formula to align the inner and the outer pinning point of a hairspring
  • Adjusting and manipulating the hairspring to match the bridge regulating device

Fine timing

  • Performing dynamic poising of the balance assembly
  • Achieving COSC chronometry standards
