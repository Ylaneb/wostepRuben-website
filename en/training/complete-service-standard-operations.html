Complete Service Standard Operations

Basics of servicing, cleaning and casing a mechanical watch.

Fundamentals needed to service a watch in a repair centre.

Training objectives

  • Complete Service Standard Operations course allows participants to develop practice and knowledge in servicing and casing a mechanical watch, in order to comply with industry standards.

Prerequisites

The Complete Service Standard Operations course is open to all participants who have attended a watchmaking school or have a minimum of 1 year full-time experience in servicing watches.

Program

Main topics

Movement servicing

  • Adjusting endshake of a barrel
  • Adjusting endshake and divisions of a gear train
  • Lubrication

Regulating organ

  • Correct handling of the balance assembly on different types of movements
  • Basic regulation
  • Respecting timing tolerances given by manufacturers

Casing

  • Performing dial and hand setting by adjusting new components
  • Proceeding to all interventions needed on the case in order to ensure waterproofness capacity.

Participant

Maximum 6 participants per session.

 

Career opportunities

With the Complete Service Standard Operations course, participants will confirm their ability to service a hand-wound mechanical watch. The fundamental skills covered by the course are mandatory for all customer services and independent repair centres.

Requirements and target audience

The Complete Service Standard Operations course is open to all participants who have attended a watchmaking school or have a minimum of 1 year full-time experience in servicing watches.
